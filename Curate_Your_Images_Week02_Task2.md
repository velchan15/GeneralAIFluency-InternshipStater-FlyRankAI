## Rejected AI-Generated Options

Three hero background textures were generated in the identity kit's palette. Two were rejected:

**Circuit-trace pattern — rejected.**
*Too literal a "generic tech startup" visual — PCB-trace patterns have become a cliché in developer/IoT portfolios. It signals "this person works in tech" without saying anything specific about me.*

**Water-ripple pattern — rejected.**
*The opposite problem: too decorative and soft for a kit built around "grounded, no fluff." Ripples are meaningful for the PureStream story specifically, but as a full-page background they pull attention toward themselves instead of supporting the content.*

**Notebook-grid texture — kept.**
*Matches the style note directly ("a working engineer's notebook, not a mood board") — it's a real object engineers actually use, not an abstract tech symbol. Low opacity keeps it calm enough that the real project captures stay the loudest thing on the page.*

## Real Photo vs. AI — Where I Made the Call

Anywhere the subject is me (profile photo, fieldwork/mentoring photos) uses a real photo — never AI-generated. Anywhere the subject is my actual work (dashboards, circuits, code, certificates) uses a real screenshot or photo. AI generation was used in exactly one place: the hero background texture, which isn't proof of anything — it's atmosphere, and it's held to the identity kit's palette so it doesn't compete with or misrepresent the real work around it.
